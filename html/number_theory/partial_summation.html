Partial Summation (or Abel's Summation)
Let \(\{a_n\}\) be a sequence of complex numbers and \(f(t)\) be a continuously differentiable function. Let \(A(t)=\sum_{n\leq t}a_n\), then \(\begin{equation} \sum_{n\leq x}a_nf(n) = A(x)f(x)-\int_1^x A(t)f^\prime(x)dt \end{equation}\)
Use the fact stated below, \[\begin{align*} \sum_{n\leq x}a_nf(n) &=\sum_{n\leq x}(A(n)-A(n-1))f(n)\\ &=\sum_{n\leq x}A(n)f(n) - \sum_{n\leq x-1}A(n)f(n+1)\\ &=\sum_{n\leq x-1}A(n)(f(n)-f(n+1))+A(x)f(x)\\ &=A(x)f(x)+\sum_{n\leq x-1}A(n)\int_{n+1}^{n}f'(t)dt\\ &=A(x)f(x)+\sum_{n\leq x-1}\int_{n+1}^{n}A(n)f'(t)dt\\ &=A(x)f(x)-\int_{1}^{x}A(n)f'(t)dt\\ \end{align*}\]

Remark:

  1. Here \(a_n\) is an Arithmetic function
  2. Fact: \(a_n=A(n)-A(n-1)\)
  3. When \(x\notin\mathbb{N}\), then \(\begin{equation} A(x)f(x)-f(\lfloor x \rfloor)-\int_{\lfloor x \rfloor}^{x} A(t)f'(t)dt=0 \end{equation}\)

More General Version
Let \(0\leq \lambda_1< \lambda_2 < ... \) be an increasing real sequence and \(C_n\) be a complex sequence. Let \(C(t)=\sum_{\lambda_n\leq t}C_n\) and \(f(t)\) be a continuous derivative, then for \(x\geq\lambda_1\), \(\begin{equation} \sum_{\lambda_n\leq x}C_nf(\lambda_n) = C(x)f(x)-\int_{\lambda_1}^x C(t)f^\prime(x)dt \end{equation}\) and if \(C(x)f(x)\rightarrow0\) as \(x\rightarrow\infty\), then \(\begin{equation} \sum^{\infty}_{n=1}Cnf(\lambda_n)=-\int_{\infty}^{\lambda_1}C(t)f'(t)dt \end{equation}\)
