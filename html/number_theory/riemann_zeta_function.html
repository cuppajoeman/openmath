Riemann Zeta-Function
For \(s\in\mathbb{C}\) with \(\Re(s)>1\), the Riemann zeta-function is defined as
For \(s>1\), \(\zeta(s)=\frac{s}{s-1}-s\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{\{x\}}{x^{s+1}}dx\). The integral converges for \(s>0\), and \(\lim_{s\rightarrow 1^+}(s-1)\zeta(s)=1\) (Zeta-Function has a simple pole at s=1 with residue 1).
