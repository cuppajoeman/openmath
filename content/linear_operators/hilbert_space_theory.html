Inner Product Space
An inner product space is a vector space \( V \) over \( \mathbb{ F } \) with a function \( \langle \cdot,\cdot \rangle: V \times V \to \mathbb{ F } \) that is positive definite, linear in one variable, and conjugate symmetric.
Hilbert Space
A Hilbert space is an inner product space that is complete with respect to the norm \( \lVert v \rVert = \sqrt{ \langle v,v \rangle } \).
Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality
Suppose that \( V \) is an inner product space and \( u,v \in V \). Then \[ \left\lvert \langle u,v \rangle \right\rvert \le \lVert u \rVert \lVert v \rVert . \]
Parallelogram Identity
Suppose that \( V \) is an inner product space and \( u,v \in V \). Then \[ \lVert u+v \rVert ^ 2 + \lVert u-v \rVert ^ 2 = 2 \lVert u \rVert ^ 2 + 2 \lVert v \rVert ^ 2 . \]
Orthogonal Complement
Suppose that \( M \subseteq H \), where \( H \) is an inner product space. The orthogonal complement of \( M \) is \[ M ^ \perp := \left\{ h \in H : \langle h,m \rangle = 0 \text{ for every } m \in M \right\}. \]
Closest Point Projection onto Closed Convex Set
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space, \( C \subseteq H \) is nonempty, closed, and convex, and \( h \in H \). Then there exists a unique \( c \in C \) such that \[ \lVert h-c \rVert = \inf _ { x \in C } \lVert h-x \rVert . \]
Orthogonal Decomposition
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space and \( M \) is a closed linear subspace of \( H \). Then \[ H = M \oplus M ^ \perp . \]
Orthonormal Family
A family \( \left( e _ j \right) _ { j \in J } \) in an inner product space is orthonormal if \( \lVert e _ j \rVert = 1 \) for every \( j \in J \), and \( \langle e _ j,e _ k \rangle = 0 \) whenever \( j \neq k \).
Bessel's Inequality
Suppose that \( \left( e _ j \right) _ { j \in J } \) is an orthonormal family in a Hilbert space \( H \). Then for every \( h \in H \), \[ \sum _ { j \in J } \left\lvert \langle h,e _ j \rangle \right\rvert ^ 2 \le \lVert h \rVert ^ 2 . \]
Parseval's Identity
Suppose that \( \left( e _ j \right) _ { j \in J } \) is an orthonormal basis of a Hilbert space \( H \). Then for every \( h \in H \), \[ \lVert h \rVert ^ 2 = \sum _ { j \in J } \left\lvert \langle h,e _ j \rangle \right\rvert ^ 2 . \]
Riesz Representation Theorem for Hilbert Spaces
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space and \( \varphi: H \to \mathbb{ F } \) is a bounded linear functional. Then there exists a unique \( h \in H \) such that \[ \varphi \left( x \right) = \langle x,h \rangle \] for every \( x \in H \).
