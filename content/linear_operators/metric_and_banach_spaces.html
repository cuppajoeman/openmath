Metric Space
A metric space is a pair \( \left( X,d \right) \), where \( X \) is a set and \( d: X \times X \to [0,\infty) \) satisfies positivity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality.
Cauchy Sequence in a Metric Space
Suppose that \( \left( X,d \right) \) is a metric space. A sequence \( x _ 1,x _ 2,\ldots \) in \( X \) is Cauchy if for every \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \), there exists \( N \in \mathbb{ Z } ^ + \) such that \( d \left( x _ j,x _ k \right) \lt \epsilon \) whenever \( j,k \ge N \).
Complete Metric Space
A metric space is complete if every Cauchy sequence in it converges to a point of the space.
Banach Fixed Point Theorem
Suppose that \( X \) is a nonempty complete metric space and \( T: X \to X \) is a contraction. Then \( T \) has a unique fixed point.
Normed Vector Space
A normed vector space is a vector space \( V \) over \( \mathbb{ F } \) with a norm \( \lVert \cdot \rVert: V \to [0,\infty) \) satisfying positivity, homogeneity, and the triangle inequality.
Banach Space
A Banach space is a normed vector space whose induced metric is complete.
Bounded Linear Map
Suppose that \( V \) and \( W \) are normed vector spaces. A linear map \( T: V \to W \) is bounded if there exists \( c \in [0,\infty) \) such that \[ \lVert T v \rVert \le c \lVert v \rVert \] for every \( v \in V \).
Operator Norm
Suppose that \( T: V \to W \) is a bounded linear map between normed vector spaces. The operator norm of \( T \) is \[ \lVert T \rVert := \sup \left\{ \lVert T v \rVert : v \in V,\ \lVert v \rVert \le 1 \right\}. \]
Linear Map Continuous iff Bounded
Suppose that \( V \) and \( W \) are normed vector spaces and \( T: V \to W \) is linear. Then \( T \) is continuous if and only if \( T \) is bounded.
Bounded Linear Maps Form a Banach Space
Suppose that \( V \) is a normed vector space and \( W \) is a Banach space. Then the space \( \mathcal{ B } \left( V,W \right) \) of bounded linear maps from \( V \) to \( W \), with the operator norm, is a Banach space.
Baire Category Theorem
A complete metric space cannot be written as a countable union of closed sets with empty interior.
Open Mapping Theorem
Suppose that \( V \) and \( W \) are Banach spaces and \( T: V \to W \) is a surjective bounded linear map. Then \( T \) maps open subsets of \( V \) to open subsets of \( W \).
Closed Graph Theorem
Suppose that \( V \) and \( W \) are Banach spaces and \( T: V \to W \) is linear. If the graph of \( T \) is closed in \( V \times W \), then \( T \) is bounded.
Uniform Boundedness Principle
Suppose that \( V \) is a Banach space, \( W \) is a normed vector space, and \( \mathcal{ A } \subseteq \mathcal{ B } \left( V,W \right) \). If \( \sup _ { T \in \mathcal{ A } } \lVert T v \rVert \lt \infty \) for every \( v \in V \), then \( \sup _ { T \in \mathcal{ A } } \lVert T \rVert \lt \infty \).
