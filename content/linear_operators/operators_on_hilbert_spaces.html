Adjoint of a Bounded Operator
Suppose that \( H \) and \( K \) are Hilbert spaces and \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H,K \right) \). The adjoint of \( T \) is the unique operator \( T ^ * \in \mathcal{ B } \left( K,H \right) \) satisfying \[ \langle T h,k \rangle = \langle h,T ^ * k \rangle \] for every \( h \in H \) and \( k \in K \).
Adjoint Exists and is Unique
Every bounded linear operator between Hilbert spaces has a unique adjoint.
Self-Adjoint Operator
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space. An operator \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \) is self-adjoint if \( T = T ^ * \).
Normal Operator
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space. An operator \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \) is normal if \( T T ^ * = T ^ * T \).
Unitary Operator
Suppose that \( H \) and \( K \) are Hilbert spaces. A surjective linear map \( U: H \to K \) is unitary if \[ \langle U h, U g \rangle = \langle h,g \rangle \] for every \( h,g \in H \).
Projection Operator
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space. An operator \( P \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \) is a projection if \( P ^ 2 = P \). It is an orthogonal projection if also \( P = P ^ * \).
Spectrum of an Operator
Suppose that \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \). The spectrum of \( T \) is \[ \sigma \left( T \right) := \left\{ \lambda \in \mathbb{ C } : T - \lambda I \text{ is not invertible} \right\}. \]
Spectrum of a Bounded Operator is Compact
Suppose that \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \). Then \( \sigma \left( T \right) \) is a nonempty compact subset of \( \mathbb{ C } \).
Spectral Radius Formula
Suppose that \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \). Then \[ \sup \left\{ \left\lvert \lambda \right\rvert : \lambda \in \sigma \left( T \right) \right\} = \lim _ { n \to \infty } \lVert T ^ n \rVert ^ { 1/n } . \]
Spectral Theorem for Compact Self-Adjoint Operators
Suppose that \( H \) is a Hilbert space and \( T \in \mathcal{ B } \left( H \right) \) is compact and self-adjoint. Then \( H \) has an orthonormal basis consisting of eigenvectors of \( T \).
