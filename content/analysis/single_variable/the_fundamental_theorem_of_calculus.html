Fundamental Theorem of Calculus I
Suppose that \( a,b \in \mathbb{ R } \), \( a \lt b \), and \( f: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is Riemann integrable. Define \( F: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) by \[ F \left( x \right) := \int _ a ^ x f. \] Then \( F \) is continuous on \( [a,b] \). If \( f \) is continuous at \( x _ 0 \in (a,b) \), then \( F \) is differentiable at \( x _ 0 \) and \[ F ^ \prime \left( x _ 0 \right) = f \left( x _ 0 \right). \]
Since \( f \) is Riemann integrable, it is bounded. Choose \( M \in \mathbb{ R } \) such that \( \left\lvert f \left( t \right) \right\rvert \le M \) for every \( t \in [a,b] \). If \( x,y \in [a,b] \) and \( x \lt y \), then by the bounds on a Riemann integral, \[ \left\lvert F \left( y \right) - F \left( x \right) \right\rvert = \left\lvert \int _ x ^ y f \right\rvert \le M \left( y - x \right). \] The same inequality with \( x \) and \( y \) interchanged gives \( \left\lvert F \left( y \right) - F \left( x \right) \right\rvert \le M \left\lvert y - x \right\rvert \), so \( F \) is continuous.

Now suppose that \( f \) is continuous at \( x _ 0 \in (a,b) \). For \( h \neq 0 \) small enough that \( x _ 0 + h \in (a,b) \),

\[ \frac{F \left( x _ 0 + h \right) - F \left( x _ 0 \right)}{h} - f \left( x _ 0 \right) = \frac{1}{h} \int _ { x _ 0 } ^ { x _ 0 + h } \left( f \left( t \right) - f \left( x _ 0 \right) \right) \, dt. \] Let \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \). Since \( f \) is continuous at \( x _ 0 \), there is \( \delta \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) such that \( \left\lvert f \left( t \right) - f \left( x _ 0 \right) \right\rvert \lt \epsilon \) whenever \( \left\lvert t - x _ 0 \right\rvert \lt \delta \). Hence, whenever \( \left\lvert h \right\rvert \lt \delta \), \[ \left\lvert \frac{F \left( x _ 0 + h \right) - F \left( x _ 0 \right)}{h} - f \left( x _ 0 \right) \right\rvert \le \epsilon. \] Therefore the derivative exists and is equal to \( f \left( x _ 0 \right) \).
Zero Derivative Implies Constant
Suppose that \( a,b \in \mathbb{ R } \), \( a \lt b \), and \( F: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is continuous on \( [a,b] \) and differentiable on \( (a,b) \). If \( F ^ \prime \left( x \right) = 0 \) for every \( x \in (a,b) \), then \( F \) is constant on \( [a,b] \).
Let \( x,y \in [a,b] \) with \( x \lt y \). By the Mean Value Theorem, there is \( c \in (x,y) \) such that \[ \frac{F \left( y \right) - F \left( x \right)}{y - x} = F ^ \prime \left( c \right) = 0. \] Since \( y - x \neq 0 \), we have \( F \left( y \right) = F \left( x \right) \). Thus \( F \) is constant.
Fundamental Theorem of Calculus II
Suppose that \( a,b \in \mathbb{ R } \), \( a \lt b \), and \( f: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is continuous. If \( F: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is continuous on \( [a,b] \), differentiable on \( (a,b) \), and \( F ^ \prime \left( x \right) = f \left( x \right) \) for every \( x \in (a,b) \), then \[ \int _ a ^ b f = F \left( b \right) - F \left( a \right). \]
Define \( G: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) by \[ G \left( x \right) := \int _ a ^ x f. \] By Fundamental Theorem of Calculus I, \( G ^ \prime \left( x \right) = f \left( x \right) \) for every \( x \in (a,b) \). Since \( F ^ \prime = f \) on \( (a,b) \), the function \( H := F - G \) has derivative \( 0 \) on \( (a,b) \). By zero derivative implies constant, \( H \) is constant. Thus \[ F \left( b \right) - G \left( b \right) = F \left( a \right) - G \left( a \right). \] Since \( G \left( a \right) = \int _ a ^ a f = 0 \) and \( G \left( b \right) = \int _ a ^ b f \), rearranging gives \[ \int _ a ^ b f = F \left( b \right) - F \left( a \right). \]
