Measure
Suppose that \( X \) is a set and \( \mathcal{ S } \) is a \( \sigma \)-algebra on \( X \). A measure on \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S } \right) \) is a function \( \mu: \mathcal{ S } \to [ 0, \infty ] \) such that \( \mu \left( \emptyset \right) = 0 \) and \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty \mu \left( E _ k \right) \] for every pairwise disjoint sequence \( E _ 1, E _ 2, \ldots \in \mathcal{ S } \).
Measure Space
A measure space is an ordered triple \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \), where \( X \) is a set, \( \mathcal{ S } \) is a \( \sigma \)-algebra on \( X \), and \( \mu \) is a measure on \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S } \right) \).
Measure Preserves Order and Set Difference
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \) is a measure space and \( D, E \in \mathcal{ S } \) with \( D \subseteq E \). Then
  • \( \mu \left( D \right) \le \mu \left( E \right) \),
  • if \( \mu \left( D \right) \lt \infty \), then \( \mu \left( E \setminus D \right) = \mu \left( E \right) - \mu \left( D \right) \).

Since \( D \subseteq E \), we can write \( E = D \sqcup \left( E \setminus D \right) \), where the union is disjoint. By the definition of a measure, \[ \mu \left( E \right) = \mu \left( D \right) + \mu \left( E \setminus D \right). \] Since \( \mu \left( E \setminus D \right) \ge 0 \), we get \( \mu \left( D \right) \le \mu \left( E \right) \). If \( \mu \left( D \right) \lt \infty \), subtracting \( \mu \left( D \right) \) from both sides gives \( \mu \left( E \setminus D \right) = \mu \left( E \right) - \mu \left( D \right) \).

Countable Subadditivity of Measures
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \) is a measure space and \( E _ 1, E _ 2, \ldots \in \mathcal{ S } \). Then \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) \le \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty \mu \left( E _ k \right). \]

Define disjoint sets \[ F _ 1 := E _ 1, \qquad F _ k := E _ k \setminus \bigcup _ { j = 1 } ^ { k - 1 } E _ j \quad \text{for } k \ge 2. \] Each \( F _ k \in \mathcal{ S } \) because \( \sigma \)-algebras are closed under standard set operations. Also \( F _ k \subseteq E _ k \), and \[ \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k = \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty F _ k. \] By countable additivity and order preservation, \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty \mu \left( F _ k \right) \le \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty \mu \left( E _ k \right). \]

Measure of an Increasing Union
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \) is a measure space and \( E _ 1 \subseteq E _ 2 \subseteq \cdots \) is an increasing sequence in \( \mathcal{ S } \). Then \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \lim _ { k \to \infty } \mu \left( E _ k \right). \]

Set \( F _ 1 := E _ 1 \) and \( F _ k := E _ k \setminus E _ { k - 1 } \) for \( k \ge 2 \). Then \( F _ 1,F _ 2,\ldots \) are pairwise disjoint, each belongs to \( \mathcal{ S } \), and \[ \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k = \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty F _ k. \] By the definition of a measure, \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty \mu \left( F _ k \right) = \lim _ { n \to \infty } \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ n \mu \left( F _ k \right). \] For each \( n \), the finite disjoint union \( F _ 1 \sqcup \cdots \sqcup F _ n \) equals \( E _ n \), so \( \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ n \mu \left( F _ k \right) = \mu \left( E _ n \right) \). Therefore \[ \mu \left( \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \lim _ { n \to \infty } \mu \left( E _ n \right). \]

Measure of a Decreasing Intersection
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \) is a measure space and \( E _ 1 \supseteq E _ 2 \supseteq \cdots \) is a decreasing sequence in \( \mathcal{ S } \). If \( \mu \left( E _ 1 \right) \lt \infty \), then \[ \mu \left( \bigcap _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \lim _ { k \to \infty } \mu \left( E _ k \right). \]

Let \[ F _ k := E _ 1 \setminus E _ k. \] Then \( F _ 1 \subseteq F _ 2 \subseteq \cdots \), and \[ \bigcup _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty F _ k = E _ 1 \setminus \bigcap _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k. \] By measure of an increasing union, \[ \mu \left( E _ 1 \setminus \bigcap _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \lim _ { k \to \infty } \mu \left( E _ 1 \setminus E _ k \right). \] Since \( \mu \left( E _ 1 \right) \lt \infty \), set-difference for finite measure gives \[ \mu \left( E _ 1 \right) - \mu \left( \bigcap _ { k = 1 } ^ \infty E _ k \right) = \lim _ { k \to \infty } \left( \mu \left( E _ 1 \right) - \mu \left( E _ k \right) \right). \] Cancelling the finite value \( \mu \left( E _ 1 \right) \) gives the desired equality.

Measure of a Union
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S }, \mu \right) \) is a measure space and \( D, E \in \mathcal{ S } \). If \( \mu \left( D \cap E \right) \lt \infty \), then \[ \mu \left( D \cup E \right) = \mu \left( D \right) + \mu \left( E \right) - \mu \left( D \cap E \right). \]

Write the union as the disjoint union \[ D \cup E = D \sqcup \left( E \setminus \left( D \cap E \right) \right). \] By finite additivity, \[ \mu \left( D \cup E \right) = \mu \left( D \right) + \mu \left( E \setminus \left( D \cap E \right) \right). \] Since \( \mu \left( D \cap E \right) \lt \infty \), set-difference for finite measure gives \[ \mu \left( E \setminus \left( D \cap E \right) \right) = \mu \left( E \right) - \mu \left( D \cap E \right). \] Substituting this into the previous equation proves the formula.
