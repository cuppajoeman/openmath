Signed Measure
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S } \right) \) is a measurable space. A signed measure on \( \mathcal{ S } \) is a countably additive function \( \nu: \mathcal{ S } \to [ -\infty,\infty ] \) that does not take both values \( \infty \) and \( -\infty \), and satisfies \( \nu \left( \emptyset \right) = 0 \).
Complex Measure
Suppose that \( \left( X, \mathcal{ S } \right) \) is a measurable space. A complex measure on \( \mathcal{ S } \) is a countably additive function \( \nu: \mathcal{ S } \to \mathbb{ C } \) satisfying \( \nu \left( \emptyset \right) = 0 \).
Positive and Negative Set for a Signed Measure
Suppose that \( \nu \) is a signed measure on \( \left( X,\mathcal{ S } \right) \). A set \( P \in \mathcal{ S } \) is positive for \( \nu \) if \( \nu \left( E \right) \ge 0 \) for every \( E \in \mathcal{ S } \) with \( E \subseteq P \). A set \( N \in \mathcal{ S } \) is negative for \( \nu \) if \( \nu \left( E \right) \le 0 \) for every \( E \in \mathcal{ S } \) with \( E \subseteq N \).
Hahn Decomposition Theorem
Suppose that \( \nu \) is a signed measure on \( \left( X,\mathcal{ S } \right) \). Then there exist disjoint sets \( P,N \in \mathcal{ S } \) such that \( X = P \cup N \), \( P \) is positive for \( \nu \), and \( N \) is negative for \( \nu \).
Mutually Singular Measures
Measures \( \mu \) and \( \nu \) on \( \left( X,\mathcal{ S } \right) \) are mutually singular, written \( \mu \perp \nu \), if there exists \( E \in \mathcal{ S } \) such that \( \mu \left( E \right) = 0 \) and \( \nu \left( X \setminus E \right) = 0 \).
Absolute Continuity of Measures
Suppose that \( \mu \) is a positive measure and \( \nu \) is a signed or complex measure on \( \left( X,\mathcal{ S } \right) \). We say that \( \nu \) is absolutely continuous with respect to \( \mu \), written \( \nu \ll \mu \), if \( \mu \left( E \right) = 0 \) implies \( \nu \left( E \right) = 0 \) for every \( E \in \mathcal{ S } \).
Jordan Decomposition Theorem
Suppose that \( \nu \) is a signed measure. Then there exist unique positive measures \( \nu ^ + \) and \( \nu ^ - \) such that \[ \nu = \nu ^ + - \nu ^ - \qquad\text{and}\qquad \nu ^ + \perp \nu ^ - . \]
Total Variation Measure
Suppose that \( \nu \) is a signed or complex measure. The total variation measure \( \left\lvert \nu \right\rvert \) is the positive measure defined by \[ \left\lvert \nu \right\rvert \left( E \right) := \sup \sum _ { k = 1 } ^ n \left\lvert \nu \left( E _ k \right) \right\rvert , \] where the supremum is over all finite measurable partitions \( E _ 1,\ldots,E _ n \) of \( E \).
Radon-Nikodym Theorem
Suppose that \( \mu \) is a \( \sigma \)-finite positive measure and \( \nu \) is a \( \sigma \)-finite positive measure such that \( \nu \ll \mu \). Then there exists a nonnegative measurable function \( f \) such that \[ \nu \left( E \right) = \int _ E f \, d\mu \] for every measurable set \( E \). The function \( f \) is unique up to equality almost everywhere.
Lebesgue Decomposition Theorem
Suppose that \( \mu \) and \( \nu \) are \( \sigma \)-finite positive measures on \( \left( X,\mathcal{ S } \right) \). Then there exist unique positive measures \( \nu _ a \) and \( \nu _ s \) such that \[ \nu = \nu _ a + \nu _ s, \qquad \nu _ a \ll \mu, \qquad \nu _ s \perp \mu . \]
