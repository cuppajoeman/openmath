Average Value over an Interval
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \) and \( I \subseteq \mathbb{ R } \) is an interval with positive finite Lebesgue measure. The average value of \( f \) over \( I \) is \[ \frac{1}{\lambda \left( I \right)} \int _ I f \, d\lambda . \]
Lebesgue Point
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 _ { \operatorname{ loc } } \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \). A point \( a \in \mathbb{ R } \) is a Lebesgue point of \( f \) if \[ \lim _ { r \downarrow 0 } \frac{1}{2r} \int _ { a-r } ^ { a+r } \left\lvert f \left( x \right) - f \left( a \right) \right\rvert \, d\lambda \left( x \right) = 0. \]
Lebesgue Differentiation Theorem
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 _ { \operatorname{ loc } } \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \). Then almost every \( a \in \mathbb{ R } \) is a Lebesgue point of \( f \).

It is enough to prove the claim for \( f\in L ^ 1\left( \mathbb{ R}\right) \), then apply it on bounded intervals for the local case. Approximate \( f \) in \( L ^ 1 \) by a continuous compactly supported function \( g \), using approximation in \( L ^ 1 \) by continuous functions. Every point is a Lebesgue point of \( g \) by continuity. The bad set for \( f \) is controlled by the Hardy-Littlewood maximal function of \( f-g \); the maximal inequality makes its measure arbitrarily small as \( \lVert f-g\rVert _1\to0 \). Hence the non-Lebesgue points of \( f \) form a null set.

Differentiation of Indefinite Integrals
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \) and \[ F \left( x \right) := \int _ { -\infty } ^ x f \, d\lambda . \] Then \( F ^ \prime \left( x \right) = f \left( x \right) \) for almost every \( x \in \mathbb{ R } \).

If \( a \) is a Lebesgue point of \( f \), then \[ \frac{F\left( a+h\right)-F\left( a\right)}{h}-f\left( a\right) = \frac{1}{h}\int_a^{a+h}\left( f-f\left( a\right)\right)d\lambda \] for \( h\gt0 \), with the analogous formula for \( h\lt0 \). The absolute value of the right side is bounded by the average of \( \left\lvert f-f\left( a\right)\right\rvert \) over an interval shrinking to \( a \), which tends to \( 0 \). By the Lebesgue differentiation theorem, this holds for almost every \( a \).

Hardy-Littlewood Maximal Function
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 _ { \operatorname{ loc } } \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \). The Hardy-Littlewood maximal function of \( f \) is \[ M f \left( x \right) := \sup _ { r \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + } \frac{1}{2r} \int _ { x-r } ^ { x+r } \left\lvert f \right\rvert \, d\lambda . \]
Hardy-Littlewood Maximal Inequality
Suppose that \( f \in L ^ 1 \left( \mathbb{ R } \right) \) and \( \alpha \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \). Then \[ \lambda \left( \left\{ x \in \mathbb{ R } : M f \left( x \right) \gt \alpha \right\} \right) \le \frac{3}{\alpha} \lVert f \rVert _ 1 . \]

For each \( x \) with \( Mf\left( x\right)\gt\alpha \), choose an interval \( I_x \) centered at \( x \) such that \( \int_{I_x}\left\lvert f\right\rvert d\lambda\gt \alpha\lambda\left( I_x\right) \). On any bounded subcollection, the one-dimensional covering lemma selects disjoint intervals \( I_j \) whose triples cover the same centers. Therefore \[ \lambda\left( \left\{ Mf\gt\alpha\right\}\cap[-N,N]\right) \le 3\sum_j \lambda\left( I_j\right) \le \frac{3}{\alpha}\int \left\lvert f\right\rvert d\lambda. \] Letting \( N\to\infty \) and using the measure of an increasing union proves the inequality.

Absolutely Continuous Function on an Interval
Suppose that \( a \lt b \). A function \( F: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is absolutely continuous if for every \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \), there exists \( \delta \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) such that for every finite pairwise disjoint collection of intervals \( (a _ k, b _ k) \subseteq [a,b] \), \[ \sum _ k \left( b _ k - a _ k \right) \lt \delta \implies \sum _ k \left\lvert F \left( b _ k \right) - F \left( a _ k \right) \right\rvert \lt \epsilon . \]
Absolutely Continuous Functions are Integrals of Their Derivatives
Suppose that \( F: [a,b] \to \mathbb{ R } \) is absolutely continuous. Then \( F ^ \prime \) exists almost everywhere, \( F ^ \prime \in L ^ 1 \left( [a,b] \right) \), and \[ F \left( y \right) - F \left( x \right) = \int _ x ^ y F ^ \prime \, d\lambda \] whenever \( a \le x \le y \le b \).

Absolute continuity implies bounded variation, so \( F \) is differentiable almost everywhere and \( F'\in L ^ 1\left( [a,b]\right) \). Define \[ G\left( x\right)=F\left( a\right)+\int_a^x F'\,d\lambda. \] By differentiation of indefinite integrals, \( G'=F' \) almost everywhere. Thus \( H=F-G \) is absolutely continuous with derivative \( 0 \) almost everywhere. Applying the absolute-continuity condition to the open set where \( H \) changes by more than a fixed amount shows \( H \) is constant. Since \( H(a)=0 \), \( H=0 \), giving the displayed integral formula.
